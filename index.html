The Berlin Compiler Meetup

The Berlin Compiler Meetup is an informal event focused on discussing compiler construction, programming language design, and related topics. It takes place about once per month.

Next meetup: Monday July 1st.

We meet in the upstairs (5th floor) space at co.up [info]. Presentations start at 19:30.

At Adalbertstraße 8, near Kottbusser Tor, there's an entrance to a set of office buildings. The door is in the back. Be sure to press the Co.up doorbell for the 5th, not the 3rd floor. Climb up five floors, and you'll find us there.

Join the mailing list for announcements and discussion related to this meetup.

See also the reading club that grew out of this meetup. Communication for the reading club happens on the mailing list linked above.

Talks for the next event

No confirmed talks. If no talks are announced, we will simply discuss a bunch of language design topics.

Did you study the internals of some compiler or VM? Are you working on a toy language? Read some mind-blowing papers on a related subject? Any other war stories to share? Submit a talk! Simply write to the mailing list with your proposal. We'll schedule it for the next meeting.

The format of the talks is 20 to 30 minutes, plus discussion. There'll be a beamer (VGA port) to connect your laptop to if you want to, but talking without slides is perfectly acceptable.
